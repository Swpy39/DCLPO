# DiffCurri-DPO: Difficulty-Aware Curriculum for Direct Preference Optimization


Large Language Models (LLMs) trained with large-scale web data have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across a variety of scenarios of the World Wide Web, such as information retrieval and knowledge graph construction. A key algorithm for training LLMs is Direct Preference Optimization (DPO). DPO leverages preference pairs as training data to align models with human preferences and has emerged as a strong alternative to Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback (RLHF). Recently, many studies have attempted to integrate curriculum learning into DPO to further enhance its ability to capture human preferences. However, existing methods mostly rank responses within the same prompt, overlooking difficulty variations across prompts and the fine-grained differences among triplets. This limits alignment effectiveness. We propose DiffCurri-DPO, a difficulty-aware curriculum framework for preference alignment. Our method introduces a multidimensional difficulty assessment model that jointly considers preference-pair distinguishability and prompt complexity. Difficulty is quantified via distributional divergence and semantic entropy, yielding a comprehensive triplet-level difficulty score. Guided by these scores, DiffCurri-DPO employs an adaptive difficulty dynamic calibration mechanism through the dynamic modulation of the DPO temperature parameter $\beta$, creating a progressive curriculum that smoothly adapts to increasing difficulty. On MT-Bench, DiffCurri-DPO substantially outperforms both vanilla DPO and curriculum-enhanced variants. In addition, Compared with existing methods, DiffCurri-DPO achieves outstanding performance on the SHP-2 and UltraFeedback test sets.
